More Destinations

Explore more amazing destinations with Wanderlust!

Kashmir Valley

Kashmir Valley in India is famed for its stunning landscapes, including the serene Dal Lake and Mughal Gardens. This region captivates visitors with its snow-capped mountains, vibrant flower gardens, and historic houseboats drifting on the water. It offers opportunities for trekking and skiing amidst lush valleys and meadows, making it a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Rajasthan Desert

The Rajasthan Desert in India is renowned for its vast expanse of golden sands, dotted with ancient forts and palaces. It offers camel safaris, traditional music, and cultural festivals like the vibrant Pushkar Fair. This arid landscape captivates visitors with its rich history and stunning sunset views, making it a quintessential destination for experiencing Rajasthan's cultural richness and natural beauty.

Ladakh

Ladakh, nestled in the Himalayas, is known for its breathtaking landscapes, including rugged mountains, serene lakes, and high-altitude deserts. It offers unique cultural experiences with its Buddhist monasteries, vibrant festivals, and traditional villages. Adventure seekers can enjoy trekking, river rafting, and mountain biking. The stark beauty of Pangong Lake and Nubra Valley makes Ladakh a must-visit destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike.

Andaman Islands

The Andaman Islands offer a tropical paradise with pristine beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs perfect for diving and snorkeling. Visitors can explore the rich history at Cellular Jail and enjoy water sports at Radhanagar Beach. The islands are also home to diverse wildlife and lush rainforests, providing ample opportunities for eco-tourism. The serene environment and beautiful sunsets make the Andaman Islands an ideal destination for relaxation and adventure.

Darjeeling

Darjeeling, nestled in the Himalayas, is famed for its tea gardens and scenic views of Mount Kanchenjunga. The Toy Train offers a unique way to explore the region. Visitors can enjoy vibrant local culture, colonial architecture, and serene monasteries, making it a charming retreat.

Uttarakhand

Kedarnath is the most remote of the four Chota Char Dham pilgrimage sites. It is located in the Himalayas, about 3,583 m (11,755 ft) above sea level near the Chorabari Glacier, which is the source of the Mandakini river. The town is flanked by snow-capped peaks, most prominently the Kedarnath Mountain..

Maharashtra

Maharashtra is a diverse state known for its bustling cities and rich history. Mumbai, the capital, is famous for Bollywood, Gateway of India, and Marine Drive. Pune offers cultural landmarks and educational institutions. The state is also home to the Ajanta and Ellora Caves, UNESCO World Heritage Sites, and vibrant festivals.

Assam

Assam, located in northeastern India, is renowned for its tea gardens, producing some of the world's finest teas. The state is home to the Kaziranga National Park, famous for its one-horned rhinoceros. The Brahmaputra River, one of the major rivers in Asia, flows through Assam, providing stunning landscapes. Assam also boasts rich cultural heritage with traditional music, dance, and festivals like Bihu.

Himachal Pradesh

Himachal Pradesh, nestled in the Himalayas, is known for its breathtaking landscapes and serene hill stations like Shimla, Manali, and Dharamshala. The state offers numerous adventure activities such as trekking, paragliding, and skiing.

Pondicherry

Pondicherry, also known as Puducherry, is a picturesque coastal city that reflects a unique blend of French and Indian cultures. Stroll along its serene beaches, explore the charming French Quarter with its colonial architecture, and visit spiritual retreats like Auroville and Sri Aurobindo Ashram.

Meghalaya

Meghalaya, the "abode of clouds," boasts lush hills, stunning waterfalls like Nohkalikai, and unique living root bridges. Shillong offers music festivals, while Mawlynnong charms with its cleanliness and Khasi culture. Trek the Khasi Hills, experience matrilineal traditions, and taste local delights like Jadoh, making Meghalaya a haven for nature lovers and culture seekers.

Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as Kashi, is a sacred city on the banks of the Ganges River in India, renowned for its spiritual significance, ghats, and vibrant culture.Varanasi is also famous for its ancient temples, bustling narrow lanes, and vibrant evening Ganga Aarti ceremonies.

Madhya Pradesh

Known for its rich cultural heritage, Madhya Pradesh boasts of historic landmarks like Khajuraho Temples and Sanchi Stupa, along with wildlife sanctuaries like Bandhavgarh and Kanha National Parks. It's also home to the stunning Marble Rocks at Bhedaghat on the Narmada River.

Gujarat

Gujarat is renowned for its vibrant culture, the white desert of Kutch, historic sites like the Sabarmati Ashram, and diverse cuisine including Gujarati thali. Its coastline along the Arabian Sea offers beautiful beaches and the famous Gir National Park, home to Asiatic lions.

Karnataka

Karnataka offers a mix of ancient temples (like Hampi and Belur), modern tech hubs in Bangalore, scenic hill stations like Coorg, and serene beaches in Gokarna. The state is also known for its rich biodiversity in places like the Western Ghats and Nagarhole National Park.

Kolkata

Kolkata, the cultural capital of India, is known for its colonial architecture, artistic heritage, Durga Puja celebrations, and mouthwatering Bengali cuisine. It's also a hub for literature and intellectual discussions, with notable landmarks like the Victoria Memorial and Howrah Bridge.

Odisha

Odisha is famous for its ancient temples such as the Konark Sun Temple and Jagannath Temple, pristine beaches like Puri Beach, and vibrant tribal cultures. The state's lush greenery and wildlife sanctuaries like Simlipal National Park add to its natural beauty and biodiversity.
